## 🔬 **Scientific Research-Based Standards**

### **Protein Requirements (ISSN Standards)**

#### **For Weight Loss:**
- **1.6-2.2g per kg body weight** (higher to preserve muscle)
- **35% of total calories** from protein
- **Example**: 75kg person → 120-165g protein daily

#### **For Muscle Gain:**
- **1.6-2.2g per kg body weight** (for muscle synthesis)
- **30% of total calories** from protein  
- **Example**: 75kg person → 120-165g protein daily

#### **For Maintenance:**
- **0.8-1.2g per kg body weight** (for maintenance)
- **25% of total calories** from protein
- **Example**: 75kg person → 60-90g protein daily

### **Fat Requirements**

#### **Essential Minimum:**
- **0.8g per kg body weight** (minimum for hormone production)
- **20-30% of total calories** from fat
- **Example**: 75kg person → 60g fat minimum

### **Carbohydrate Requirements**

#### **Minimum for Brain Function:**
- **130g minimum daily** (glucose for brain)
- **35-45% of total calories** from carbs
- **Complex carbs preferred** over simple sugars

## 🎯 **Goal-Specific Strategies**

### **1. Weight Loss (Cutting)**
```
Protein: 35% (1.4g per kg) - preserves muscle mass
Fat: 30% (0.8g per kg minimum) - hormone production  
Carbs: 35% - energy for workouts
```

**Why this works:**
- Higher protein prevents muscle loss during calorie deficit
- Moderate fat maintains hormone levels
- Lower carbs encourage fat burning

**Why this works:**
- Balanced approach for sustainable maintenance
- Adequate protein for tissue repair
- Sufficient carbs for daily activities

## 📈 **Activity Level Multipliers (Harris-Benedict Formula)**

### **Scientifically Accurate:**
- **Sedentary**: BMR × 1.2 (desk job, no exercise)
- **Light**: BMR × 1.375 (light exercise 1-3 days/week)
- **Moderate**: BMR × 1.55 (moderate exercise 3-5 days/week)
- **Active**: BMR × 1.725 (hard exercise 6-7 days/week)
- **Very Active**: BMR × 1.9 (very hard exercise, physical job)

## 🧮 **Calculation Examples**

### **Example 1: 75kg Male, 30 years, 175cm, Moderate Activity, Goal: Lose Weight**

#### **BMR Calculation (Mifflin-St Jeor):**
```
BMR = (10 × 75) + (6.25 × 175) - (5 × 30) + 5
BMR = 750 + 1094 - 150 + 5 = 1699 kcal
```

#### **TDEE with Activity:**
```
TDEE = 1699 × 1.55 = 2633 kcal
```

#### **Weight Loss Target:**
```
Target = 2633 - 500 = 2133 kcal
```

#### **Macro Distribution:**
```
Protein: (2133 × 0.35) ÷ 4 = 187g (2.5g per kg) ✅
Fat: (2133 × 0.30) ÷ 9 = 71g (0.95g per kg) ✅  
Carbs: (2133 × 0.35) ÷ 4 = 187g ✅
```

### **Example 2: 60kg Female, 25 years, 165cm, Active, Goal: Gain Muscle**

#### **BMR Calculation:**
```
BMR = (10 × 60) + (6.25 × 165) - (5 × 25) - 161
BMR = 600 + 1031 - 125 - 161 = 1345 kcal
```

#### **TDEE with Activity:**
```
TDEE = 1345 × 1.725 = 2320 kcal
```

#### **Muscle Gain Target:**
```
Target = 2320 + 300 = 2620 kcal
```

#### **Macro Distribution:**
```
Protein: (2620 × 0.30) ÷ 4 = 197g (3.3g per kg) ✅
Fat: (2620 × 0.25) ÷ 9 = 73g (1.2g per kg) ✅
Carbs: (2620 × 0.45) ÷ 4 = 295g ✅
```

## 🏥 **Health Safety Limits**

### **Minimum Daily Requirements:**
- **Protein**: 0.8g per kg (WHO minimum)
- **Fat**: 0.8g per kg (essential fatty acids)
- **Carbs**: 130g (brain glucose requirement)

### **Maximum Safe Limits:**
- **Protein**: 2.2g per kg (upper safe limit)
- **Fat**: 1g per kg (maximum for heart health)
- **Carbs**: No strict limit, but <300g recommended
